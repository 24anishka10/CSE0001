# 🛡️Digital Safety Checklist
**UPI : Never Enter PIN to Receive Money :** This is the most common scam. Legitimate payments to your account do not require your UPI PIN, an OTP, or a QR code scan. If a "buyer" (e.g., on OLX) asks you to scan a code to "receive" funds, it is a scam to deduct money from your account.

**UPI : Verify "Collect Requests" Carefully :** Before hitting "Approve" on any payment notification, check the recipient's name and the amount. Scammers often send "Collect Requests" with urgent messages like "Refund Processing" to trick you into authorizing an outgoing payment.

**Beware of "Digital Arrest" Scams :** Scammers may call pretending to be police or CBI officials, claiming your Aadhaar or phone is linked to a crime and placing you under a fake "digital arrest". Fact: No Indian law enforcement agency will arrest you or conduct an investigation over a WhatsApp or Skype video call.

**Enable Biometric & Multi-Factor Authentication :** Move beyond simple passwords. Enable Fingerprint/Face ID for all banking and social media apps. Under new RBI 2026 rules, a single OTP is no longer enough; ensure you have a second independent factor like a PIN or biometric enabled.

**Scrutinize "Lucrative" Job Offers :** Be wary of high-paying "work from home" jobs found on Telegram or WhatsApp that require an initial "registration fee" or "security deposit". Real companies, including major Indian firms, will never ask for money to hire you.

**Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks :** Avoid using free Wi-Fi at cafes or campus canteens for banking or shopping. Hackers can set up "Evil Twin" hotspots to intercept your data. Use your mobile data or a trusted Virtual Private Network (VPN) for these transactions.

**Verify Official Customer Care via Apps :** Never search for customer care numbers on Google or social media comments, as these are often fake numbers planted by scammers. Always use the "Help" or "Support" section directly inside the official app.

**Ignore Fake "KYC/SIM Blocked" Alerts :** If you receive an SMS claiming your SIM or bank account will be blocked unless you click a link to "update KYC," ignore it. Genuine KYC updates are only done through official bank apps or by visiting a physical branch.
# 🚨 How to Report Cybercrime
- If you suspect you have been scammed, the first 2 hours are critical for potentially freezing the fraudulent transaction.

- Call the National Helpline: Dial 1930 immediately.

- File an Online Complaint: Report the incident on the official National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal.

- Notify Your Bank: Contact your bank's official customer care to block your cards and accounts.
